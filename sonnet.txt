Sonnet for a Running Editor

When Claude may reach through protocol and wire
To touch the buffer where your cursor bides,
And fold a line, or search with pattern's fire,
Or send to terminals on either sides —

No longer must the model guess at state,
Or blindly write to files it cannot see;
The active view is read, the symbols wait,
And build output confirms what used to be.

Two instances may run, on port and port,
One native, one in Linux kernel space;
Mirrored networking makes the loopback short,
And both ST and Claude find their own place.

  So here's a sonnet, typed by distant hand —
  The MCP bridge worked exactly as planned.
